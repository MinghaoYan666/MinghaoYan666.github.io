全文链接：

A new fracture simulation algorithm based on peridynamics for brittle objects

文章摘要：

Fracture simulation can create amazing effects in motion pictures, it is widely used into video games and virtual reality systems. In order to achieve a fast and realistic fracture simulation and in view of the advantages of peridynamics in solving discontinuity problems, we propose a new model, which is based on spring mass model and peridynamics theory, for brittle objects fracture. The method can be described as a two-step strategy. First, the geometric model of an object is preprocessed. The model is completely wrapped in an Axis-Aligned Bounding Box (AABB) envelop box, which is subdivided into multiple fragments with 3d Voronoi diagram. The intersection of fragments and the model surface is computed by using the Boolean algorithm, and the fragments outside the model surface are clipped to obtain the final fragments. The Binary Space Partitioning (BSP) tree subdivide method is adopted in our preprocessing, which further improves the intersection speed. Second, the fracture is calculated according to spring-mass system based on peridynamics. We view each point as a spring node. The seed points are connected according to the rules of the spring-mass model based on peridynamics to form the spring topology. The object fracture is calculated according to the spring-mass model based on peridynamics. To further improve render quality, normal bump texture mapping is employed to render the fracture surface. Experimental results show that the proposed model provides users with improved visual feedback while the computational cost is at the same magnitude of other similar methods. The proposed method is especially suitable for the simulation of brittle object fracturing.

二、以负责人的身份，参加国家级大学生创新训练项目

项目名称：基于深度学习的脑肿瘤图像分割方法研究
项目编号：202210403070
项目链接：基于深度学习的脑肿瘤图像分割方法研究

项目内容：

研究一种基于深度学习的三维MRI脑肿瘤分割方法，结合分割对象的低级特征和高级特征，有效融合多尺度信息和全局上下文信息从而减少学习到的冗余特征的影响，以达到精确分割脑肿瘤效果。

项目特色：

本项目提出一种联合多尺度上下文和全局上下文的深度卷积网络，能够同时捕获不同尺度的空间信息和长距离的特征依赖性。多尺度卷积联合模块和全局上下文聚合模块能够整合全局和局部的特征信息，结合分割对象的低级特征和高级特征，有效融合全局上下文信息并减少了学习到的冗余特征的影响，最终提高了网络对目标区域的分割能力。

项目成就：

1、设计了一种用于脑肿瘤分割的深度学习网络；2、申请了一项发明专利。

项目技术方案：

首先，对三维MRI脑部数据进行预处理并划分数据集；

MRI图像有4个不同的模态包括T1、T1ce、T2和FLAIR，我们把4个数据拼接在一起形成4个输入通道，将原始大小为155240240的三维脑部MRI图像裁切至150192192大小，去除多余的背景像素。在输入网络前对数据进行归一化处理，降低网络的学习难度，并采取在线的方式对训练数据进行多样化操作，包括随机缩放，沿三维方向随机翻转和随机裁切，最终送入网络的三维图像大小为96144144。最后将大脑数据集按8：1：1的比例划分为训练集、验证集和测试集。

然后，构建并训练了神经网络

利用所述训练集对所述的深度卷积神经网络进行训练并利用所述验证集随时对训练的网络进行验证。其中，所述的深度卷积神经网络有一个编码器和对应的解码器，解码器通过跳跃连接获取编码器的特征。
经过预处理的数据首先输入到包含3组下采样卷积模块和1组多尺度卷积联合模块的编码器中进行编码，之后，完成编码的特征图输入到由3个全局上下文聚合模块构成的解码器中进行解码，最终输出分割结果。

最后对分割结果后处理

将测试数据送入训练好的深度卷积神经网络模型进行预测，输出的特征图经过后处理后，得到最终的肿瘤。通常增强肿瘤区域较难预测，容易产生假阳性的预测结果，所以当预测的增强肿瘤区域过小时，我们将增强肿瘤区域替换为坏死/水肿区域。

1.美国大学生数学建模竞赛 （MCM/ICM）

担任队长 获得成绩:24年5月公布

竞赛内容：

对于北美五大湖复杂的水系情况和水系中的利益相关者，提出一种水坝的控制算法使得能够最大程度满足实际情况和水系周围的利益相关者。

竞赛工作：

构建五大湖的网络模型；拟合各利益相关者的效益函数；引入天气因素对水位的干扰，并计算敏感度；构造水坝在成本计算约束下的控制算法，使得对利益相关者的效益总和比历史真实值高 23.3%

论文摘要：

Great Lakes of the United States and Canada are the largest group of freshwater lakes in the world. Great lakes and their interconnected waterways form a huge watershed, and the water of these lakes is widely used for a variety of purposes, with many different stakeholders. The water level of each lake depends on the amount of water input and output and other weather and geomorphological factors.
First, we model the lake network and model the intricate water system of Great Lakes. For different stakeholders, we need to build the first model to consider the overall utility maximization under different water flows and water levels. What’s more, we have two dam devices that can control the flow of water. For these two dams, we built a second model, a control algorithm to solve the problem of maximum utility brought by the water level, so that the best control scheme is considered under the constraints of control cost.
In the first model, we establish the utility function of each stakeholder, calculate the utility weight of different stakeholders through the tax amount, and use the different − evolution algorithm to find the water level and flow under the global maximum amount. and compare the utility at historical water levels.
In the second model, we build a sub-model of water level and water flow, climate and other related factors, and use historical correlation data to calculate the regression equation. Then, a cost function is established, and under the constraint of the cost function, the differential equation is used to obtain the optimal water level under control. The superiority of our control algorithm and its sensitivity to weather factors are calculated.
In the first model, we can bring 37.8% more utility than the historical real water level. The second model, under cost constraints, can bring 23.3% more utility than the real historical data, and the model can get close to the ideal water level of model I after one year of regulation. The regression value of the model changes by 17.8% under the disturbance of 5% given to the weather factor, which is affected by certain weather conditions, which is in line with the actual situation. Our model can bring greater overall utility to different stakeholders in Great Lakes, and can take into account more control costs, providing more convenience to dam managers.
